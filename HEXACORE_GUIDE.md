# 💎 HEXACORE — How to Play

> **Build words. Spawn gems. Level up. Climb higher.**
> Hexacore is an endless word-building game played on a living hexagonal grid. Every word you find transforms the board — spawning gem tiles that multiply your score and fuel your level progression.

---

## 🎯 The Objective

Your goal is simple: **keep leveling up**.

Level up by earning score. Earn score by building words. Build better words to spawn more powerful gem tiles. Use gem tiles **in** your words to earn even more score. The cycle never stops — but the board keeps evolving, so neither can you.

There is no timer. There is no word limit. The only thing standing between you and the next level is your vocabulary.

---

## 🕹️ The Basics

### The Board
Hexacore is played on a **hexagonal grid**. Every tile on the board holds a letter. Tiles have point values — common letters are worth less, rare letters are worth more.

### Building a Word
- **Tap or click** a starting tile to begin your selection.
- **Drag or tap adjacent tiles** to chain letters into a word.
- Every tile you include must be **directly adjacent** to the previous one — you cannot skip tiles.
- Words must be **at least 4 letters long**.
- Submit the word when you're ready. If it's valid, the tiles are consumed and the board refills from above — like gravity.

### After You Submit
Once a word is accepted:
1. The used tiles **pop off the board** with a burst animation.
2. Remaining tiles **fall down** to fill the gaps (gravity).
3. New letter tiles **drop in from above** to refill.
4. **Gem tiles spawn** on the board as your reward.

---

## 📈 Leveling Up

Your **level** is driven entirely by your **score**. Every word you submit adds to your running total, and each level requires progressively more score to reach.

When you hit a level threshold, a **LEVEL UP!** banner flashes across the screen — and the challenge intensifies.

> 💡 *The path to high levels runs through long words and gem tile chains. Short words keep you alive — long words push you forward.*

---

## 💎 Gem Tiles

Gem tiles are the heart of Hexacore's scoring engine. They appear on the board as a **reward for the words you build** — the longer the word, the more (and rarer) gems spawn.

When you **include a gem tile in a word**, it contributes a significantly higher score value than a normal tile. Chaining multiple gems into a single word is where truly massive scores come from.

> 🔑 **Key insight:** Gems don't just give you score — they *invite* you to build words *around* them, which in turn spawns even more gems.

---

## 💠 Gem Spawn Rewards

The gem tiles that spawn after each word depend entirely on **word length**. Longer words spawn more gems, and rarer gem types only appear at greater word lengths.

| Word Length | Gems Spawned |
|:-----------:|:-------------|
| **4 letters** | 1 Emerald |
| **5 letters** | 2 Emeralds |
| **6 letters** | 3 Emeralds · 1 Gold |
| **7 letters** | 3 Emeralds · 2 Gold · 1 Sapphire |
| **8 letters** | 4 Emeralds · 3 Gold · 2 Sapphire · 1 Pearl |
| **9 letters** | 5 Emeralds · 4 Gold · 3 Sapphire · 2 Pearl · 1 Tanzanite |
| **10+ letters** | 6 Emeralds · 5 Gold · 4 Sapphire · 3 Pearl · 2 Tanzanite · 1 Ruby |

> 🔥 **Ember tiles** double the gem reward — every gem spawn fires *twice* when an Ember is included in your word.

## 💎 The Gems — A Visual Field Guide

Each gem type has a distinct color, rarity, and visual identity on the board. Here's what to look for:

---

### 🟢 Emerald
![Emerald Gem](https://placehold.co/80x80/22c55e/ffffff?text=💎&font=noto-emoji)

> *The gateway gem. Spawns from even the shortest valid words.*

The most common gem on the board, Emerald is your bread and butter. You'll see it constantly — and that's by design. Emeralds are the engine that keeps your score climbing between big plays. Include them freely; they reward consistent play.

---

### 🟡 Gold
![Gold Gem](https://placehold.co/80x80/eab308/ffffff?text=💛&font=noto-emoji)

> *Where real momentum begins. Appears once your words hit 6 letters.*

Gold tiles are a clear signal that your word-building is leveling up. They carry noticeably more weight than Emeralds and start appearing in meaningful numbers once you commit to longer words. Stacking Gold into a word alongside other gems is a reliable way to push a score spike.

---

### 🔵 Sapphire
![Sapphire Gem](https://placehold.co/80x80/3b82f6/ffffff?text=💙&font=noto-emoji)

> *The mid-tier gem. 7-letter words unlock the Sapphire.*

Sapphire tiles signal that you're playing at an advanced level. They're valuable enough to actively plan around — spotting a cluster of Sapphires and threading a long word through them can dramatically accelerate your level progress.

---

### 🤍 Pearl
![Pearl Gem](https://placehold.co/80x80/e2e8f0/1a1a1a?text=🤍&font=noto-emoji)

> *Rare. Elegant. Only the longest common words will summon it.*

Pearls arrive with 8-letter words and represent a meaningful leap in rarity. A Pearl on the board is an invitation to pause and plan — find a path through it and you'll feel the score difference immediately. When you chain Pearls with other high-tier gems, the results are substantial.

---

### 🟣 Tanzanite
![Tanzanite Gem](https://placehold.co/80x80/8b5cf6/ffffff?text=💜&font=noto-emoji)

> *Reserved for elite play. Needs a 9-letter word to appear.*

Tanzanite is a rare gem that marks you as a serious Hexacore player. Getting one on the board requires finding a 9-letter word — no small feat. But once it's there, it's a high-value target that can anchor an entire scoring run if you build around it.

---

### 🔴 Ruby
![Ruby Gem](https://placehold.co/80x80/ef4444/ffffff?text=❤️‍🔥&font=noto-emoji)

> *The pinnacle of the gem tier. Only 10+ letter words can summon a Ruby.*

Ruby is the rarest gem you can spawn through word length alone. A 10-letter word is a genuine achievement in Hexacore, and the Ruby tile it produces reflects that. Including a Ruby in any subsequent word is one of the most impactful single-tile plays in the game. If you see one — plan for it.

## 🔥 Special Tiles

Beyond gems, the board occasionally features **special tiles** that change how the game plays.

---

### 🔥 Ember
> *A burning tile that amplifies everything.*

When an Ember tile is included in your word, your **entire gem reward fires twice**. Every gem that would have spawned spawns again. Ember tiles are rare and precious — using one on a long word creates an explosive chain of gems on the board.

---

### 🌀 Prism
> *A wildcard that bends the rules.*

Prism tiles carry special behavior when consumed. They interact with the board's refill and scoring systems in unique ways, creating opportunities for unexpected plays.

---

### ᚱ Rune
> *Ancient. Powerful. Handle with care.*

Rune tiles are enigmatic high-value tiles with their own visual identity and scoring weight. Including a Rune in a word unlocks its hidden potential.

---

### ◈◉ Portal
> *Teleport your word path across the board.*

After every 10th word you submit, a **Portal** opens between two corner tiles — one marked **◈** (entry) and one marked **◉** (exit). When both portal tiles are included in the same word, your path can **jump** from the entry directly to the exit, allowing word chains that would otherwise be impossible. The portal stays open for 3 words, then closes.

---

## 🏆 Tips for High Scores

- **Think in layers.** Your next word should use the gems your last word spawned.
- **Long words over short ones — always.** A 7-letter word doesn't just score more; it seeds the board with far better tiles.
- **Plan around Ember.** If an Ember appears, hold off on submitting until you've built your longest possible word through it.
- **Use the Portal.** Corner-to-corner connections open word paths that the rest of the board can't offer. Don't waste the window.
- **Don't ignore short words.** 4 and 5 letter words keep the board refreshed and keep Emeralds flowing when you're setting up a bigger play.

---

*Build long. Collect gems. Level up. That's Hexacore.*
